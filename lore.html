📜 Lore of Norrath

🏛 A Brief History of Norrath

The Age of Creation

In the beginning, the universe was formless — a vast void of infinite possibility. Into this void came the gods, beings of pure energy and will who shaped reality according to their desires. Veeshan, the Crystal Wurm and Queen of all Dragonkind, was among the first to claim a world. She descended on the newly formed sphere of Norrath and raked her great claws across its northern continent, leaving behind the Ring of Scale — her children, the dragons — as both claim and custodian.

Other gods followed. Brell Serilis, the Duke of Below, retreated into the world's depths and created the dwarves, gnomes, and other subterranean races to populate his domain. Tunare, the Mother of All, shaped the forests and created the elves from the living wood itself. Cazic-Thule, the Face of Fear, gave form to lizardmen and trolls in the swamps and jungles, creatures whose existence embodied primal terror. And Rallos Zek, the Warlord, created the ogres and giants and sent them forth to make war upon all others — a decision that would ultimately lead to one of the greatest catastrophes in Norrath's history.

The Age of War and the Rathe Curse

The armies of Rallos Zek, led by the ogres in their full ancient power, invaded the Plane of Earth — the domain of the Rathe Council, council of gods who governed the physical world. This unprecedented transgression of mortal armies into divine territory enraged the gods. The Rathe Council, acting in concert with other divine powers, laid a terrible curse upon the invaders: the ogres were stripped of their intelligence, reduced from brilliant generals and philosophers to the dull brutes they remain today. The armies were shattered and driven back. The giants were similarly diminished, and the greater war engine of Rallos Zek was broken.

But the damage was done. The barrier between the mortal world and the planes had been breached by mortal ambition, and the gods grew wary. Divine investment in Norrath's affairs became more cautious, more mediated, and the age of direct god-mortal interaction began its long decline.

The Iksar Empire

On the continent of Kunark, the Iksar — a race of intelligent lizard-folk — rose to create the greatest empire Norrath had yet seen. At their peak, the Iksar Empire spanned the entire continent of Kunark and maintained active campaigns against the froglok populations of Antonica. Their cities were marvels of architecture, their armies a terror, and their scholars the most prolific historians of their era.

The empire's fall was as spectacular as its rise. Internal civil wars, a growing undead plague that their own necromancers could not fully control, and the resurgence of the Ring of Scale dragons who had endured Iksar expansion for too long — all converged simultaneously. When the dust settled, the Iksar Empire was a ruin. The survivors scattered, and it would be centuries before the Iksar people began the long process of rebuilding what would become the city of Cabilis.

The Modern Age

In the current era, the great powers of Norrath exist in uneasy balance. Freeport and Qeynos maintain their ancient rivalry on Antonica. The elves of Faydwer press against the expanding orc empires. Kunark's jungles are contested between the reforming Iksar civilization and countless adventurers from across the world. Velious was only recently rediscovered after centuries of isolation behind magical storms. And Luclin, the moon, has become accessible through the great Nexus spires — revealing a civilization of Vah Shir cat-people and the deeply unsettling ruins of the Akheva who served the moon goddess directly.

"We stand at a crossroads of history. The forces that shaped the old world — the empires, the wars, the gods themselves — are stirring again. What comes next will be written in the deeds of those bold enough to act." — Maelin Starpyre, Grand Librarian of Myrist

✨ The Pantheon of Norrath

The Triumvirate of Fate

Above all other gods stand three primordial forces: Veeshan, who claimed Norrath in the age of creation; The Nameless, the primordial void from which all things emerged; and The Faceless, a counter-force to creation whose nature is not fully understood even by the gods themselves.

The Major Deities

Tunare — The Mother of All. Goddess of nature and growth, creator of the elves. She is the most widely worshipped benevolent deity, revered by druids, rangers, and many who draw power from the natural world. Her temples are living gardens.

Solusek Ro — The Burning Prince. God of fire and the sun, son of the solar god Fennin Ro. The kobolds of the Lavastorm Mountains revere him as their divine patron. His priests command fire magic of extraordinary power.

Innoruuk — The Prince of Hate. God of hatred and darkness, creator of the dark elves. His theology teaches that hate is the truest form of strength and that all other emotions are weakness. His church is politically sophisticated and deeply dangerous.

Cazic-Thule — The Face of Fear. God of fear, terror, and undead. Creator of the trolls, lizardmen, and other fear-touched races. His temples are deliberately designed to maximize psychological terror, and his priests are among the most feared in Norrath.

Rallos Zek — The Warlord. God of war, creator of the ogres and giants. His theology is simple: strength is the only virtue, and the strong deserve to rule. The Rallosian cult has never forgiven the gods for the curse laid on their people.

Brell Serilis — The Duke of Below. God of underground places and creation, father of the dwarves, gnomes, and deep races. His church emphasizes craftsmanship, endurance, and the value of things built to last.

Mithaniel Marr — The Truthbringer. God of valor and truth, patron of the half-elves. His paladins are among the most widely respected warriors on Norrath. His theology holds that truth and courage, even when painful, are always superior to comfortable lies.

Erollisi Marr — Queen of Love. Goddess of love and compassion, twin sister of Mithaniel. Her church emphasizes sacrifice, connection, and the redemptive power of love in a world full of hatred and war.

Bristlebane Fizzlethorpe — King of Thieves. God of trickery, luck, and mischief. Beloved by rogues, gamblers, and the desperate. He is the only major deity who seems genuinely amused by mortal follies.

Karana — The Rainkeeper. God of weather and plains. Ancient deity revered by the farmers and herders of Antonica's interior. His storms are both blessing and curse to those who live under the open sky.

Prexus — The Ocean Lord. God of the seas and deep waters. Revered by sailors, fishermen, and those whose lives depend on the ocean. His influence is felt strongly in the ports of Norrath and on every vessel that crosses the deep.

Quellious — The Tranquil. God of peace and serenity. The rarest of divine philosophies in a world of constant conflict. Her monks achieve extraordinary focus and power through absolute mental discipline.

Veeshan — The Crystal Wurm. Not worshipped in the traditional sense, but revered by dragons and acknowledged by scholars as the first claimant of Norrath. Her relationship to mortal races is one of profound indifference at best, contempt at worst.

👥 The Races of Norrath

Human

The most numerous and adaptable of the mortal races, humans have built the largest cities on Antonica — most notably Freeport and Qeynos, which represent opposing political philosophies as much as geography. Humans are the only race with no divine claim to a specific creator, a fact that gives them both freedom and a kind of spiritual restlessness that drives their ambition.

High Elf

The eldest of the elven kindreds, created directly by Tunare to be her most perfect expression of beauty and magical attainment. High elves of Felwithe are notable for their exceptional magical ability and for an aristocratic reserve that other races frequently experience as condescension. Their history of thousand-year lifespans has given high elven culture a patience and long-term perspective that humans often find disorienting.

Wood Elf

A younger branch of the elven family, wood elves sacrificed the high elves' magical refinement for an intimate connection with the natural world. They are the finest archers in Norrath and move through wilderness terrain with supernatural ease. Their treetop city of Kelethin is one of the architectural wonders of Faydwer.

Dark Elf

The most contentious origin story in elvenkind — dark elves are elves transformed by Innoruuk after the god kidnapped and remade two of Tunare's creations as his own. Dark elves are brilliant, beautiful, and deeply dangerous. Their underground city of Neriak contains some of the most sophisticated political intrigue in Norrath, and their magical practitioners are regarded as among the most powerful in the known world.

Dwarf

Created by Brell Serilis to inhabit and steward the deep places of the earth, dwarves are compact, immensely strong, and possess a cultural stubbornness that borders on the supernatural. Their cities — Kaladim in Faydwer and Thurgadin in Velious — are engineering masterpieces carved directly from living rock. Dwarven craftsmanship, particularly in metalwork, is acknowledged as the finest on Norrath.

Gnome

Also created by Brell Serilis, but where dwarves received strength and endurance, gnomes received a restless mechanical intelligence. The gnomes of Ak'Anon have built a civilization of clockwork wonders that astonishes every first-time visitor. Their curiosity about how things work extends to a dangerous interest in necromantic research that their leadership officially discourages but privately enables.

Halfling

Small, cheerful, and deceptively capable, halflings have built their community of Rivervale in the lush hills of Antonica. They are regarded by larger races as harmless, which suits them perfectly. Halfling rogues and druids are among the most underestimated adventurers in Norrath.

Iksar

The intelligent lizard-folk of Kunark, descendants of an empire that once dominated an entire continent. The Iksar are proud to the point of arrogance, deeply xenophobic, and possessed of a cultural memory of greatness that drives both their ambition and their bitterness. Their rebuilt city of Cabilis is a fortress as much as a community, and the militia that guards it has no tolerance for perceived weakness.

Vah Shir

A feline race from Luclin, the moon of Norrath. The Vah Shir were transported to the moon long ago under circumstances they only partially understand, and have built a civilization adapted to the moon's unique conditions. They are fierce warriors with a strong clan-based social structure and an innate pride that sometimes causes friction with the many races that have begun arriving via the Nexus spires.

Goliath

The hardy race of Goliaths, or Northmen, use their wisdom and formidable brawn to defend honor and justice. Their magnificent stature is a sight to behold; even more so when it is unleashed in battle. Barbarians are one of the tallest races to walk the lands of Norrath.

As a tribal culture, Goliaths are a little rough around the edges when it comes to interactions with other races. Even the Goliaths' mannerisms and features have a rugged quality.

The Goliaths consider themselves superior to other inhabitants of Norrath, particularly the Teir`Dal and Erudites, which are viewed as pathetically weak. Goliaths have a special place in their hearts for the Dwarves of Kaladim whom they view as steadfast and brave. The high quality of Dwarven mead may also have some influence on the cordial relationship between the two races. Ogres, Trolls, Dark Elves, and the other evil races are hated, as they directly oppose the Goliaths' sense of justice.

Goliaths live in the frigid city of Halas, nestled amidst the icy peaks of Everfrost in Northern Antonica. The city is rather rudimentary, but serves the needs of the Barbarians that call it home.

⚔️ Major Factions of Norrath

The City of Freeport

Governed by the Overlord Lucan D'Lere, a former paladin who executed his own superior and seized control of the city through an act of violence that shocked Norrath. Freeport presents a facade of law and order, but beneath it operates a deeply corrupt system where the Militia is above the law and dissent is dangerous. Freeport maintains trade relationships with dark elves, trolls, and other traditionally hostile races — pragmatism over principle is the city's unofficial motto.

The City of Qeynos

Governed by the Bayle family, whose patriarch Kane Bayle represents a more traditional concept of noble governance. Qeynos is broadly aligned with good-aligned races and maintains hostile relationships with Freeport's darkness. The Circle of Unseen Hands — a thieves' guild — operates within Qeynos in a complicated relationship with the city guard, sometimes enemies, sometimes convenient allies against external threats.

The Ring of Scale

The governing body of dragonkind, based in the Temple of Veeshan on Velious. The Ring of Scale has existed since the first age of Norrath and considers itself the rightful steward of the world Veeshan claimed. Their relationship to mortal civilizations oscillates between distant contempt and active hostility, depending on whether those civilizations are encroaching on draconic territory.

The Combine Empire (Historical)

A now-fallen human empire of extraordinary reach, the Combine once managed the West Commonlands as neutral territory and promoted a multi-racial civilization of unusual tolerance. Its collapse — the reasons for which are debated by scholars — left behind ruins, infrastructure, and a cultural memory of a more integrated Norrath that some idealists still invoke as a model.

The Coldain

The dwarven clan of Velious, isolated from their Faydwer cousins for centuries behind the continent's magical storms. The Coldain have developed independently, with their own traditions, leaders, and deep-rooted hatred of the giants of Kael Drakkal who have been their enemies since the first days of Velious settlement. Dain Frostreaver IV currently leads them from the underground city of Thurgadin.

The Iksar Empire (Reformed)

The modern Iksar government centered in Cabilis is simultaneously a continuation of the ancient empire and something entirely new — a traumatized civilization rebuilding with the memory of past glory and the weight of catastrophic failure. The various sects within Cabilis represent different philosophies about how that rebuilding should proceed, and their internal conflicts occasionally turn violent.

🌌 Cosmology — The Structure of Existence

The universe of Norrath is organized into three broad tiers of existence: the Mortal Realm (Norrath and its moon Luclin), the Elemental Planes (domains of the primal forces), and the Higher Planes (the personal domains of the major deities).

The Mortal Realm

Norrath is the physical world — the continent cluster of Antonica, Faydwer, and Odus; the distant continent of Kunark; the frozen southern continent of Velious; and the moon Luclin. These locations are connected by ocean travel, druidic teleportation, and the Nexus spires erected on Luclin.

The Elemental Planes

Below the higher divine planes but above the mortal realm exist the great elemental planes: Earth, Air, Fire, and Water. Each is the domain of a corresponding deity and is populated by elemental beings who are native to those conditions. The planar barriers between these realms and the mortal world are permeable but require significant magical effort to cross — or direct divine permission.

The Higher Planes

The personal domains of the major gods — the Plane of Hate, the Plane of Fear, the Plane of Growth, the Plane of Mischief — occupy a tier of reality beyond the elemental planes. These locations are defined entirely by their ruling deity and operate according to that deity's nature rather than any consistent physical law. The Plane of Knowledge, unusually, is not a deity's personal domain but rather a neutral nexus — the Library of Myrist — that exists between planes as a place of learning and connection.

Beyond even the highest divine planes lies the Plane of Time, which exists outside of all normal cosmological structure. It is here that the barriers between mortal and divine achievement become most theoretical — and most dangerous.

"Three tiers of gods rule our existence. The first tier — the creators — shaped the world itself. The second — the sustainers — maintain what was made. The third — the actors — intervene directly in mortal affairs. And then there is a possible fourth tier, a new manifestation previously unknown to mortal ideals of the cosmos. The scholars who traveled to investigate this possibility have not returned." — Maelin Starpyre
