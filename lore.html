📜 Lore of Norrath

🏛 A Brief History of Norrath

The Age of Creation

In the beginning, the universe was formless — a vast void of infinite possibility. Into this void came the gods, beings of pure energy and will who shaped reality according to their desires. Veeshan, the Crystal Wurm and Queen of all Dragonkind, was among the first to claim a world. She descended on the newly formed sphere of Norrath and raked her great claws across its northern continent, leaving behind the Ring of Scale — her children, the dragons — as both claim and custodian.

Other gods followed. Brell Serilis, the Duke of Below, retreated into the world's depths and created the dwarves, gnomes, and other subterranean races to populate his domain. Tunare, the Mother of All, shaped the forests and created the elves from the living wood itself. Cazic-Thule, the Face of Fear, gave form to lizardmen and trolls in the swamps and jungles, creatures whose existence embodied primal terror. And Rallos Zek, the Warlord, created the ogres and giants and sent them forth to make war upon all others — a decision that would ultimately lead to one of the greatest catastrophes in Norrath's history.

The Age of War and the Rathe Curse

The armies of Rallos Zek, led by the ogres in their full ancient power, invaded the Plane of Earth — the domain of the Rathe Council, council of gods who governed the physical world. This unprecedented transgression of mortal armies into divine territory enraged the gods. The Rathe Council, acting in concert with other divine powers, laid a terrible curse upon the invaders: the ogres were stripped of their intelligence, reduced from brilliant generals and philosophers to the dull brutes they remain today. The armies were shattered and driven back. The giants were similarly diminished, and the greater war engine of Rallos Zek was broken.

But the damage was done. The barrier between the mortal world and the planes had been breached by mortal ambition, and the gods grew wary. Divine investment in Norrath's affairs became more cautious, more mediated, and the age of direct god-mortal interaction began its long decline.

The Iksar Empire

On the continent of Kunark, the Iksar — a race of intelligent lizard-folk — rose to create the greatest empire Norrath had yet seen. At their peak, the Iksar Empire spanned the entire continent of Kunark and maintained active campaigns against the froglok populations of Antonica. Their cities were marvels of architecture, their armies a terror, and their scholars the most prolific historians of their era.

The empire's fall was as spectacular as its rise. Internal civil wars, a growing undead plague that their own necromancers could not fully control, and the resurgence of the Ring of Scale dragons who had endured Iksar expansion for too long — all converged simultaneously. When the dust settled, the Iksar Empire was a ruin. The survivors scattered, and it would be centuries before the Iksar people began the long process of rebuilding what would become the city of Cabilis.

The Modern Age

In the current era, the great powers of Norrath exist in uneasy balance. Freeport and Qeynos maintain their ancient rivalry on Antonica. The elves of Faydwer press against the expanding orc empires. Kunark's jungles are contested between the reforming Iksar civilization and countless adventurers from across the world. Velious was only recently rediscovered after centuries of isolation behind magical storms.

"We stand at a crossroads of history. The forces that shaped the old world — the empires, the wars, the gods themselves — are stirring again. What comes next will be written in the deeds of those bold enough to act." — Maelin Starpyre, Grand Librarian of Myrist

👥 The Species of Norrath

Human

The most numerous and adaptable of the mortal races, humans have built the largest cities on Antonica — most notably Freeport and Qeynos, which represent opposing political philosophies as much as geography. Humans are the only race with no divine claim to a specific creator, a fact that gives them both freedom and a kind of spiritual restlessness that drives their ambition.

Erudite

A highly advanced offshoot of humanity, descended from scholars who isolated themselves on the continent of Odus centuries ago. Their sustained focus on magical study across generations has produced a race of tall, nearly hairless humans with dramatically enlarged craniums — physical evidence of accelerated mental development. The Erudites founded the city of Erudin, a center of arcane learning without peer in the known world, and their mages and clerics consistently rank among the most powerful practitioners in Norrath. This intellectual refinement comes with a corresponding social coldness; Erudites are widely perceived as arrogant, and their internecine conflicts carry a peculiar intensity, as evidenced by the schism that split their society and produced the renegade dark practitioners of Paineel.

High Elf

The eldest of the elven kindreds, created directly by Tunare to be her most perfect expression of beauty and magical attainment. High elves of Felwithe are notable for their exceptional magical ability and for an aristocratic reserve that other races frequently experience as condescension. Their history of thousand-year lifespans has given high elven culture a patience and long-term perspective that humans often find disorienting.

Wood Elf

A younger branch of the elven family, wood elves sacrificed the high elves' magical refinement for an intimate connection with the natural world. They are the finest archers in Norrath and move through wilderness terrain with supernatural ease. Their treetop city of Kelethin is one of the architectural wonders of Faydwer.

Dark Elf

The most contentious origin story in elvenkind — dark elves are elves transformed by Innoruuk after the god kidnapped and remade two of Tunare's creations as his own. Dark elves are brilliant, beautiful, and deeply dangerous. Their underground city of Neriak contains some of the most sophisticated political intrigue in Norrath, and their magical practitioners are regarded as among the most powerful in the known world.

Dwarf

Created by Brell Serilis to inhabit and steward the deep places of the earth, dwarves are compact, immensely strong, and possess a cultural stubbornness that borders on the supernatural. Their cities — Kaladim in Faydwer and Thurgadin in Velious — are engineering masterpieces carved directly from living rock. Dwarven craftsmanship, particularly in metalwork, is acknowledged as the finest on Norrath.

Gnome

Also created by Brell Serilis, but where dwarves received strength and endurance, gnomes received a restless mechanical intelligence. The gnomes of Ak'Anon have built a civilization of clockwork wonders that astonishes every first-time visitor. Their curiosity about how things work extends to a dangerous interest in necromantic research that their leadership officially discourages but privately enables.

Halfling

Small, cheerful, and deceptively capable, halflings have built their community of Rivervale in the lush hills of Antonica. They are regarded by larger races as harmless, which suits them perfectly. Halfling rogues and druids are among the most underestimated adventurers in Norrath.

Iksar

The intelligent lizard-folk of Kunark, descendants of an empire that once dominated an entire continent. The Iksar are proud to the point of arrogance, deeply xenophobic, and possessed of a cultural memory of greatness that drives both their ambition and their bitterness. Their rebuilt city of Cabilis is a fortress as much as a community, and the militia that guards it has no tolerance for perceived weakness.

Vah Shir

A feline race from Luclin, the moon of Norrath. The Vah Shir were transported to the moon long ago under circumstances they only partially understand, and have built a civilization adapted to the moon's unique conditions. They are fierce warriors with a strong clan-based social structure and an innate pride that sometimes causes friction with the many races that have begun arriving via the Nexus spires.

Goliath

The hardy race of Goliaths, or Northmen, use their wisdom and formidable brawn to defend honor and justice. Their magnificent stature is a sight to behold; even more so when it is unleashed in battle. Goliaths are one of the tallest races to walk the lands of Norrath. As a tribal culture, Goliaths are a little rough around the edges when it comes to interactions with other races. Even the Goliaths' mannerisms and features have a rugged quality.

Froglok

An amphibious people of remarkable piety and quiet resilience, the Froglok were shaped by Mithaniel Marr, the God of Valor, as a counterpoint to the brutish creatures that dominate Norrath's swamps and wetlands. For much of recorded history the Froglok were enslaved by the Trolls of Grobb, held in the cavern-warrens of the Guk ruins beneath Innothule Swamp — ancient remnants of their own civilization repurposed as a prison. Their long captivity did not break them; it refined a culture of patience, collective endurance, and deep faith that makes the Froglok formidable despite their unassuming appearance. Those who have won their freedom travel Norrath as healers, warriors, and scholars, carrying a quiet dignity that consistently surprises those who expect less. They have reclaimed portions of the Guk ruins as the settlement of Gukta, a fragile but proud foothold for a people determined to reclaim what was taken from them.

Orc

The fractious children of Rallos Zek, created to wage war and nothing else. Organized into competing warbands and tribal confederacies across Antonica and beyond, orcs have never achieved the unified civilization their numbers would suggest they could — what they lack in organization they compensate for in relentless aggression. Their encampments expand steadily into surrounding territory, and the ongoing pressure against human settlements in the plains of Karana and along the Qeynos approaches represents one of the defining background threats of the current era. The Crushbone clan on Faydwer is the most organized of their kind, their hierarchy guided by dark elf advisors who lend strategic sophistication to what would otherwise be pure, undirected brutality.

Goblin

Goblins occupy the ecological niche of opportunistic scavengers in Norrath's monster hierarchy — numerous, adaptable, and possessed of a low cunning that makes them more dangerous than their size suggests. They appear in caverns, ruined structures, and wilderness areas across Antonica and Faydwer, establishing warrens wherever they find defensible ground and a reliable food source. Goblins rarely build lasting institutions, but their numbers ensure they persist as a background threat in virtually every region. The Runnyeye Citadel in Antonica represents one of their more ambitious settlements — a subterranean complex that has changed hands multiple times between goblin factions, its ownership never stable for long.

Troll

Massive, regenerating, and possessed of an appetite that extends to anything organic, trolls are among the most feared creatures of the Norrath lowlands. Their city of Grobb sits deep in Innothule Swamp, and it is from this festering base that they have historically raided surrounding settlements and held the froglok people in subjugation for centuries. A troll's most distinctive attribute — rapid regeneration of wounds that would kill any other creature — makes them extraordinarily difficult to put down, and adventurers who underestimate the time required to finish one reliably do not make that mistake twice. Troll society has its own hierarchy and its own spiritual practices centered on Cazic-Thule, and their political competition, while expressed entirely through violence, is no less complex for it.

Ogre

The ogres were once the most brilliant military minds on Norrath — generals and tacticians of Rallos Zek's armies who planned and executed the invasion of the Plane of Earth itself. That hubris cost them everything. The curse levied by the Rathe Council stripped ogres of their intelligence, reducing a race of philosophers and commanders to the slow, powerful brutes they are today. Their city of Oggok on Antonica is governed entirely by the principle that might determines standing, with hierarchy enforced through violence alone. Individual ogres retain flashes of the spatial awareness and tactical instinct that made their ancestors terrifying, but lack the capacity to coordinate these instincts into anything beyond a charging assault — a tragedy written in flesh that the ogres themselves are no longer equipped to understand.

⚔️ Major Factions of Norrath

The City of Freeport

Governed by the Overlord Lucan D'Lere, a former paladin who executed his own superior and seized control of the city through an act of violence that shocked Norrath. Freeport presents a facade of law and order, but beneath it operates a deeply corrupt system where the Militia is above the law and dissent is dangerous. Freeport maintains trade relationships with dark elves, trolls, and other traditionally hostile races — pragmatism over principle is the city's unofficial motto.

The City of Qeynos

Governed by the Bayle family, whose patriarch Kane Bayle represents a more traditional concept of noble governance. Qeynos is broadly aligned with good-aligned races and maintains hostile relationships with Freeport's darkness. The Circle of Unseen Hands — a thieves' guild — operates within Qeynos in a complicated relationship with the city guard, sometimes enemies, sometimes convenient allies against external threats.

The Ring of Scale

The governing body of dragonkind, based in the Temple of Veeshan on Velious. The Ring of Scale has existed since the first age of Norrath and considers itself the rightful steward of the world Veeshan claimed. Their relationship to mortal civilizations oscillates between distant contempt and active hostility, depending on whether those civilizations are encroaching on draconic territory.

The Combine Empire (Historical)

A now-fallen human empire of extraordinary reach, the Combine once managed the West Commonlands as neutral territory and promoted a multi-racial civilization of unusual tolerance. Its collapse — the reasons for which are debated by scholars — left behind ruins, infrastructure, and a cultural memory of a more integrated Norrath that some idealists still invoke as a model.

The Iksar Empire (Reformed)

The modern Iksar government centered in Cabilis is simultaneously a continuation of the ancient empire and something entirely new — a traumatized civilization rebuilding with the memory of past glory and the weight of catastrophic failure. The various sects within Cabilis represent different philosophies about how that rebuilding should proceed, and their internal conflicts occasionally turn violent.

🌌 Cosmology — The Structure of Existence

The universe of Norrath is organized into three broad tiers of existence: the Mortal Realm (Norrath and the realms beyond), the Elemental Planes (domains of the primal forces), and the Higher Planes (the personal domains of the major deities).

The Mortal Realm

Norrath is the physical world — the continent cluster of Antonica, Faydwer, and Odus; the distant continent of Kunark; and the frozen southern continent of Velious. These locations are connected by ocean travel and druidic teleportation.

The Elemental Planes

Below the higher divine planes but above the mortal realm exist the great elemental planes: Earth, Air, Fire, and Water. Each is the domain of a corresponding deity and is populated by elemental beings who are native to those conditions. The planar barriers between these realms and the mortal world are permeable but require significant magical effort to cross — or direct divine permission.

The Higher Planes

The personal domains of the major gods — the Plane of Hate, the Plane of Fear, the Plane of Growth, the Plane of Mischief — occupy a tier of reality beyond the elemental planes. These locations are defined entirely by their ruling deity and operate according to that deity's nature rather than any consistent physical law. The Plane of Knowledge, unusually, is not a deity's personal domain but rather a neutral nexus — the Library of Myrist — that exists between planes as a place of learning and connection.

Beyond even the highest divine planes lies the Plane of Time, which exists outside of all normal cosmological structure. It is here that the barriers between mortal and divine achievement become most theoretical — and most dangerous.

"Three tiers of gods rule our existence. The first tier — the creators — shaped the world itself. The second — the sustainers — maintain what was made. The third — the actors — intervene directly in mortal affairs. And then there is a possible fourth tier, a new manifestation previously unknown to mortal ideals of the cosmos. The scholars who traveled to investigate this possibility have not returned." — Maelin Starpyre

📖 Chronicles of Norrath — A Complete History

The following is a comprehensive account of Norrath's history as recorded in the great libraries of Myrist, from the void before creation through the Age of Heroes.

The Void and The Nameless

In the beginning, all that existed was void. Empty of all, whether it be time or space. A vast and endless nothingness cloaked in a deep and all-consuming silence. Within this void existed The Nameless — a power so incredible that in the entire universe, it knew only itself to exist. It had no idea why it was there, just that it was. In the quiet expanse of the void, it sent itself out and searched to find something different. And there it was: a pocket of space, and therein lay the flow of time.

When The Nameless stepped into that space, something terrible but wonderful, something terrifying but exciting happened. It detected the elements of space and time. Knowing nothing of these things, The Nameless freed what was contained there so it could spread out in all directions, filling the emptiness with a force that had no boundaries. Forces touched each other and the emptiness filled with swirling energies — light and heat touched emptiness, energies collided and crashed, were born and died and became new and different. None of those things having been seen ever before started to form planets that differed in size and structure, and stars were created to watch over this new creation. But the whole chaos needed order, shaping and fine touch. And so The Nameless created the Gods of Power.

The Seeding of Norrath

In ages past the planet of Norrath was lifeless when the goddess of the dragons, Veeshan, descended upon this world and made her mark — depositing her brood in the icy lands of Velious. For years the dragons ruled over the skies and land until the other Gods of the Pantheon became aware of the planet. Brell Serilis, God of the Underfoot, seeded the belly of Norrath with all manner of strange and wondrous creatures and then approached the other gods to create a pact. Tunare the God of Nature, Prexus the Oceanlord, and Rallos Zek the God of War were all there — all agreed except Rallos. The planet would be divided up between these powers for the purpose of keeping the Dragon Wurmqueen in check. Each would create a race of beings to watch over Norrath.

Brell claimed the bowels of the planet and created the Dwarves, stout and strong, deep beneath the mountains of Norrath. In the abysmal depths of the oceans Prexus left his children, the Kedge — hardy aquatic beings of great mental power and stamina. And on the surface of Norrath did Tunare create the Elves, creatures of limitless grace and beauty, and Rallos Zek the Giants, fierce and formidable beings intent upon the defense of their lands.

The Xulous and the Birth of Pestilence

It was inevitable that such energies involved in seeding planets with life would attract even more of the gods. An ancient race did spring forth from the will of an unknown god — the Xulous, a cunning and powerful race of beings able to survive in a harsh world ruled by the scaled children of Veeshan. The Xulous built upon the lands that would become Tunaria a great necropolis to house their dead kings, fashioned from preserved remains of slain dragons and called Lxanvon — meaning "Kings Rest" in the tongue of the Xulous.

Centuries passed and the other gods had deposited their proto-creatures among the world. It was in this time that the Goddess Anashti'Sul, ruler of the Plane of Health, in her wisdom decided to cure the ailments of death forever and granted the gift of eternal unlife to the creatures of Norrath, creating the Ewer of Sul'Dae to bestow this gift. It was a total disaster — for the first time undead roamed the world of Norrath. Despite her good intentions the other gods seized Anashti'Sul and banished her to the Void for her crimes.

Some time later, in the center of Darrow's Hollow in the Xulous kingdoms, a great black obelisk appeared as if from nowhere, spindling higher into the sky than any surrounding building. King Adan summoned all the wise men of magical learning but not one could explain its origin. Frightening creatures lurked near the city's stalls at night. The King began sending adventurers to do combat with these horrors until the day Ultor Szanvon appeared before the court, claiming to know the secrets of the obelisk. Ultor advised that King Adan's forefathers, kings of times gone by, would know the truth — and so the King brought out the ancient key to King's Rest. Only the King and Chancellor entered the crypts. There Ultor slayed King Adan and performed a dark ritual. Out of the remains of the Xulous kings that lay before him, Bertoxxulous, the God of Disease and Pestilence, was born. He conquered the Xulous and spread his armies across Norrath. In his satisfaction he transcended the Ruins of Lxanvon to the Plane of Disease, where it would forever be known as the Crypt of Decay.

The Age of Elves and the Rise of Fear

Meanwhile the world was to be graced by a new God — Cazic-Thule, Lord of Fear. After unleashing his Amygdalans and Lizardmen and constructing a grand Temple of Cazic-Thule, he began to consider a home for a new race. Far to the east, Cazic-Thule deposited his Shissar race — gigantic serpent-like creatures unparalleled in combat and the dark arts — deep within the swamps and jungles of Kunark. The Shissar thrived in their capital of Chelsith, building a mighty empire maintained by their slave race, the Iksar. It was not long before this race overstepped its boundaries, dabbling in planar travel and stealing godly artifacts. For their crimes against the pantheon, Cazic-Thule unleashed the Greenmist upon them. The deathly fog destroyed all but a few Shissar cities. In desperation, some Shissar used their knowledge of translocation and vanished entirely, while others fled deep into the underfoot.

The Elves, deposited on Norrath by Tunare, had meanwhile built a glorious empire spanning the landmass — legendary places such as the High Elven city of Fayspire and the Wood Elf city of Telethin. They named the land Tunaria in her honour. From their seat of power in Takish'Hiz in the Elddar Forest, the elves enjoyed good relations with most other races. It was not until the Elves discovered the lost shrine of Anashti'Sul — the ancient Fountain of Life and home of the fabled Ewer of Sul'Dae — that the first real threat to the elves came about. Some abandoned Tunare and began to follow this ancient god of health, creating the grand city of Ahket Aken where they lived in hope of eternal immortality.

In the shadows, the dark lord Innoruuk, God of Hate, watched over this splintered race of Elves. Using the power of the Ewer of Sul'Dae, he created his own immortal race — the Ydal — and brought them to the Plane of Hate. But a fatal flaw became evident: though immortal, they were cursed by a lust for blood, a side effect of mixing Innoruuk's blood with the Ewer. One of these Ydal was known as Mayong Mistmoore.

The Twin Gods and the Birth of the Froglok

Two new Gods arrived upon the land of Norrath — created by their father Tarew Marr: Erollisi Marr, God of Love, and Mithaniel Marr, God of Valor. Naively, in their youth, these twin gods believed they could act as the elder gods and create their own mortals. Mithaniel created the Minotaurs, intended to personify honour and courage — a decision he would later regret. Innoruuk noticed and descended from his dark Plane of Hate, attempting to corrupt Erollisi. She fled in terror to Takish'Hiz while her brother was led deep into the swamplands of Innothule, not knowing he was being led into a trap by Cazic-Thule. On his arrival he was assaulted by Lizardmen and trolls and captured by the God of Fear. Cazic-Thule's daughter, Terris-Thule, the God of Nightmares, drew from Mithaniel the Gift of Life. However, her brother Morrel-Thule — the God of Dreams — stole the Gift and broke it in two, splitting half across the swamps of Southern Tunaria. From this gift the waterborne Frogloks did arise: a valorous race who would be the saviours of their Lord Mithaniel in times ahead.

The Iksar Empire and the Age of Scale

The Iksar, freed from Shissar bondage, began to build their own empires. The first mighty tribes were the Kunzar, Jarsath, Kylong, Nathsar, and Obulus. It was one of these tribes that first made contact with Zebuxorok, the God of Knowledge — handed to Kotiz, a fabled necromancer of the Iksar, was the Unholy Writ of War, a book of godly power filled with strategies to defeat enemies and dark forgotten magic. A young Iksar named Venril Sathir would kill Kotiz, claim the Writ, and use it to dominate his Kunzar clan and annihilate the other four tribes. Venril was crowned emperor and quickly enslaved the Giants and Frogloks of Kunark, constructing the grand capital of Sebilis. A golden age began for the Iksar; new orders such as the Swiftail Caste of legendary Iksar monks were formed. As Venril grew older and wary of his own death, he made a pact with Innoruuk for immortality — all would go well until his beloved Drusella Sathir died suddenly. Venril locked her and her servants in Charasis to perform a dark ritual, which was interrupted by his son Rile Sathir, who foiled the ritual and trapped Venril within an evil amulet.

Rile Sathir took the throne and was served by legendary figures such as Chosooth the Vampire and Warlords Kurn Machta and Karatukus. Nearly all of Kunark came to belong to Sebilis. The Ring of Scale began to monitor this increasing threat. Trakanon, the large blue dragon, advocated swift intervention, but was voted down. Defying the Ring, Trakanon and an army of younger dragons descended upon Rile Sathir's fleet at sea, triggering a war that lasted nearly half a century. Atrebe Sathir took the throne and created the Sarnak — a fusion of Iksar and dragon — and the Sokokar from dragon and froglok, giving the Iksar an airborne army. His son Ganak Sathir met the war's final conclusion in an aerial duel over the Field of Bone: both Ganak and Jaled-Dar, Leader of the Ring of Scale, were incinerated. The dragons retreated after destroying the imperial palace in a final act of revenge. The remaining Iksar formed the Emerald Circle, but never returned to the grandeur of the Sathirian Empire.

The Children of Rallos Zek and the Curse of the Gods

The children of Rallos Zek — the Ogres, led by Warlord Murdunk and his aid the Sphinx Zayenaekk — built a massive army of Giants, Minotaurs, Goblins, Ogres, and Orcs that spread across Tunaria and destroyed all they crossed. Murdunk set his sights on the planes of the gods themselves. His armies translocated into the Plane of Earth but were defeated; Murdunk barely escaped. Proud of his creations' achievement, Rallos Zek came down to Norrath himself and led a second invasion with his demigod children Tallon and Vallon Zek. Their army successfully stormed the Plane of Earth and even slayed some of the Rathe Council at the site that would become Lake Rathetear. But the armies were eventually defeated and pushed back. The Elemental Gods constructed a Great Barrier, denying entry to lesser gods and mortals. Thousands of Ogres were slain, their empire collapsed, and their intelligence was stripped by the Rathe curse. The Giants were scattered from one end of the earth to the other, their lush homelands buried under snow and ice. The Goblins were also cursed, though what writing remains of their punishment is scant — they no longer keep records of their history, which some argue may itself be some indication of the severity of what was taken from them. Some Giants, attempting to escape the curse, fled far south and crossed the frigid barrier to Velious, settling deep in the dragon lands.

The Dragons of Velious — Kerafyrm the Sleeper

The Kromzek Giants of Velious went to war with the dragons, and a constant struggle ensued until a blasphemous event occurred: two dragons of different colours mated and gave birth to a prismatic offspring named Kerafyrm. This mating was against the laws of Veeshan. Kildrukaun, Arch Priest of Veeshan, ordered the parents be put to death and appointed himself custodian of the powerful prismatic dragon. A splinter group of dragons sought to oversee and control the newer races being deposited by other gods; they formed The Ring of Scale and left for other lands. Kildrukaun, furious at the division, prophesied that Kerafyrm would lead the Claws of Veeshan in a great civil war against the Ring of Scale, forcing reunification and fulfilling Veeshan's will. Kerafyrm ascended quickly within the Claws of Veeshan, proving as shrewd as he was merciless. But no battle satiated the creature's unrivaled lust for destruction. Eventually the Council of Elders was forced to order his imprisonment.

The Coldain Dwarves and the Spine of Solusek Ro

Meanwhile, Erollisi bore child and traveled far to the Northlands with the elves of Tunaria, giving birth to the Goliath race among the snowy tundra and wastes of the giants' fallen empires. The Frogloks of the swamps rose up in continuous efforts to save Mithaniel Marr from the clutches of Cazic-Thule — many fell and their efforts were futile, but they never gave up hope. Some years on, the Goliaths, driven by their love for the Marr twins, reinforced the Frogloks and finally saved Mithaniel Marr, vanquishing the Avatar of Fear — a manifestation of Cazic-Thule himself — from Norrath.

From Faydwer, the Dwarves ventured out into the world. A scouting party led by Colin Dain, intended to establish a quartz mine on Odus, was caught in a storm and left stranded on Velious among the Kromzek Giants and the Claws of Veeshan. Colin Dain's child, Dain Frostreaver, created the outpost of Froststone — but it was soon found and attacked by the Kromzek. Colin Dain sacrificed himself to protect his people. From this point the Velious dwarves called themselves the Coldain and their leaders Dain in his honour. The Crystal Caverns became their home for a time until further Kromzek attacks pushed them into the Great Divide, led by Dain Frostreaver II. There they founded New Froststone, which they would later name Thurgadin.

Throughout all this, the Burning Prince Solusek Ro, child of Fennin Ro the God of Fire, had watched over Norrath with a hot temper. He looked down on Tunaria, the land of the elves, and decided to make his mark — raising the land around the Elddar Forest and arching the great Serpent Mountains, bringing heat and sun to the ancient forest. This event would forever doom the elves of Tunaria and the lands surrounding it despite the efforts of their King and Queen Tak'Yaliz.

The Fall of the Elves and the Rise of the Humans

After the effects of Solusek Ro, the rivers of Tunaria ran dry and it rained less each year. The prophetic Queen Elizerain came to power but even the elven druids could only delay the inevitable. Slowly the forest gave way to desert, and eventually even the fair elven city of Takish'Hiz crumbled. The elves were forced to retreat from Tunaria, leaving much of their greatness behind.

It was during this time that a young Goliath named Bayle Shiverfist headed south from the northlands where the Goliaths had been created by Erollisi. They traveled past the evacuated dwarven mines of Blackburrow and settled in the Karanas, a place they named Oceangreen. The Gnolls of the Jaggedpine Forest also found a new home in the remains of Blackburrow. Bayle Shiverfist stumbled upon an ancient artifact deep underwater — the Staff of Theer — in the remains of a goblin city. On returning it to the elders of his clan it unleashed great chaos, and mysterious void storms began occurring all over the Plains of Karana. For the trouble he caused he began a great quest with the gnome Twiddy Bobick aboard the legendary airship Cloudskipper. The Trolls of Innothule also suffered from the void storms in this era; one of their residents, Kruzz Skullcleaver, would eventually join Bayle on this grand journey. They would meet with the Ring of Scale and eventually Queen Elizerain, who was leading the last of the elves from Tunaria across the waters to their new home on Faydwer. There the elves settled a new city named Felwithe.

Back on Tunaria, the Goliaths' civilization advanced far beyond what it had been. They became cultured and civilised, and their settlements became towns and cities. Legendary places were formed such as Highpass Hold, Highbourne, and Freeport, and trade flourished among the tribes. The Goliaths became known as humans. They ventured all over Tunaria and across the sea, creating places such as Tanaan — a legendary temple and home to those forsaken from their gods. The use of magic increased, geomancy was studied by humans for the first time, and Zan Fi created the Whistling Fist Clan, traveling the world teaching the ideals of its order. As towns grew they joined together, and eventually a new Combine Empire was formed, led by Tsaph Katta.

The Combine Empire

As the Empire grew it came into contact with most races of the world, including the Teir'Dal. Some dark elves came and studied with the humans, including the mage Valdoartus Varsoon, who became the first to solve the Ember Conundrums in the Archesian Order on his first attempt — and revealed an eighth Conundrum besides. For his efforts he was granted access to Planar Thaumaturgy and began opening portals to the planes, bringing back all manner of artifacts including the ivory teacup and the Whisper of Marr, an egg that burns anything to a crisp.

A golden age for many races on Norrath followed. Captain Han Jensen discovered Faydwer; Zan Fi's monastic order gained many followers; new orders formed including the Ashen Order, the Silent Fist Clan, and the Swifttail Caste on Kunark. Zan Fi, perhaps as a gift from the gods, received a vision of a serene island where he was to build a grand tower. Following a mysterious song as his guide, he arrived at the island where the city of forsaken souls, Tanaan, had been built, and there he formed the Lost Circle. The Gnomish people of Ak'Anon also enjoyed a boom in inventions, creating a magical clockwork King to rule over their people and delivering them from an ancient conflict. It seemed that war was finally at an end for all of Norrath.

Wishing to formalize relations with other races, Tsaph Katta hosted a grand banquet to invite the darker races to join the Combine Empire. However, Seru, leader of the Combine Army, poisoned Tsaph during this banquet and blamed the dark elves — beginning a great inquisition against dark magic and the evil races. It would be the darkest time for the Combine Empire and also its final destruction. The Empire split: loyalists of Tsaph fled to every corner of Norrath while Tsaph Katta's body was led away to a secret location by the Elves. Eventually, having nowhere else to run, the Loyalists stumbled upon an ancient Shissar city in the Dreadlands of Kunark and used the ancient translocation structure to vanish entirely. Seru, furious, followed soon after — leaving the Combine Empire to finally fall without a leader.

The Age of Enlightenment

Vallius Bayle and Sir William Taros were born, and many great adventures were undertaken across Norrath. Vallius Bayle discovered the fabled weapon Soulfire in the Teir'Dal Duchy of the Dead and located the Qeynos Claymore, which he presented to the human clans of the Plains of Karana. Great tournaments known as the Gauntlet were held, and the claymore was granted to the victor who would lead the people. Sir William Taros formed the Order of Marr, which Vallius Bayle would join. Vallius eventually had a son named Antonius Bayle, who would later win the Gauntlet and unite the clans of the plains, forming the City of Qeynos — earning the title The Great Unifier.

On Luclin, the Town of Shadowhaven was formed by the remnants of the Combine Empire, a subterranean city spanning the dark corridors of the Nexus. Lcea Katta formed Katta Castellum far away from Seru in the Tenebrous Mountains; Seru himself formed Sanctus Seru and eventually placed himself in stasis until Tsaph Katta was found.

On Kunark, the Amulet of Venril Sathir was located and returned to Charasis, and a new Iksar empire formed in Cabilis under Emperor Vekin.

On Tunaria, the first shadow knight was created in Freeport through a pact with Innoruuk. Freeport struggled against Deathfist Orc and Dark Elf attacks in a battle known as the Long Night, eventually overcome by the many orders of Freeport led by an elf named Aataltaal — who then abandoned them to find their own ruler. In Qeynos, the ancient dark elf Valdoartus Varsoon caused great trouble, culminating in the burning of the Keep of Immortality. Antonius Bayle II was crowned and petitioned to rename Tunaria to Antonica among all the human races.

In the south, mysterious creatures called the Jal'Raeth studied the ancient ruins of the Xulous Empire and became obsessed with its plagued remains. They flocked to Castle Envar and became overwhelmed by disease. One renegade of their kind, a pacifist named Rodcet Nife, sought to heal and enhance all manner of races throughout the realms. For his actions he ascended to godhood, becoming the new God of Health to replace Anashti'Sul. He descended on Qeynos in his strange circular craft and helped heal their plagues and disease, resulting in the formation of the Celestial Watch and the many orders that would worship Rodcet Nife, the Prime Healer.

In Faydwer, the first Ayr'Dal — Half Elf — was born to Amber and Fendel in Highpass. HighKeep was founded by Sean McCabe. Erud was born and led a group of scholars across the ocean to found Arcadin far to the west on the land of Odus. Arcadin was renamed Erudin in Erud's honour, and the Erudites eventually split into two factions; one began to study necromancy in a city they named Paineel. The resulting magical war between the factions was so catastrophic that it blew a hole in the land of Odus so vast it created the great chasm known simply as The Hole.

During this era the mage Miragul mastered all three schools of magic and began to dabble with the dark arts. Using his unrivaled skills of illusion he slipped in and out of both Erudin and Paineel with ease and eventually double-crossed Erudin, stealing all manner of artifacts. He is said to have visited the ancient vampire Mayong Mistmoore and engaged with The Dead of Neriak. Miragul eventually settled in the lands of the north, plaguing the Goliaths during his time there, building his menagerie in search of an answer to his own mortality — and ultimately becoming trapped within a phylactery of his own making.

The Age of Turmoil

Antonius Bayle III became known as the Great Diplomat for increasing relations with the rival city of Erudin and aiding with tensions between the erudites and heretics. He fathered several sons: Kyle Bayle, charged with extending the borders of Qeynos to the south; Antonius Bayle IV, who would eventually take the crown; and Kane Bayle. Mithaniel Marr also descended on Norrath, calling on the people to wage war against the rising undead in Faydwer in what became known as the Crusade of Tears. This left Lucan D'Lere, a Knight of Truth, in charge of Freeport while the armies were away. Lucan became corrupt, murdered Sentry Dilius, and refused to hand power back to the Knights of Truth when they returned — causing an eternal conflict between the knights and Lucan's new militia that would define Freeport for generations.

Antonius Bayle IV became romantically involved with Lady Shae, though the relationship was eventually broken off by the Circle of Ten and the elves of Felwithe. Shortly after these events Antonius Bayle III — the Great Diplomat — passed away, and Antonius Bayle IV was crowned king, ushering in the Age of Turmoil.

Shortly after his crowning, Kane Bayle, brother of Antonius, was made Captain of the Guard where he became lulled into involvement with the Bloodsabers, a dark clan of Bertoxxulous worshippers deep under the city of Qeynos. He plotted endlessly with them for the control of Qeynos while Antonius remained blissfully unaware. The alliance between the Bloodsabers and Valdoartus Varsoon bore dark fruit: a devastating plague swept across the Karana, claiming countless lives. Though adventurers managed to defeat Varsoon, he ultimately escaped. The Elves of Faydwer attempted their second landing on the continent of Kunark and established a city named Firiona Vie. Firiona Vie was captured by Danak but eventually rescued by the great Erudite wizard Al'Kabor and others. The Dark Elves also established a city in the remains of an old Iksar settlement on Kunark's far shore.

Gnomish pirates discovered a passage through the frigid barrier with a grand ship they named The Icebreaker, and soon many races began to travel to Velious, where relations were built between the Coldain of Thurgadin and the Kromzek of Kael Drakkal.

The Age of Heroes

And so begins the Age of Heroes. It is an age named not for some antiquated sorcerer or long-lived warlord, but for you. Too long has the world gone without a group of champions willing to risk their lives for the greater good. Tragedies, turmoil, and turbulence thrive in every corner of these lands — but I am certain that you are but one of the mighty heroes who will set us back on the path of progress.

"We stand at a crossroads of history. The forces that shaped the old world — the empires, the wars, the gods themselves — are stirring again. What comes next will be written in the deeds of those bold enough to act." — Maelin Starpyre, Grand Librarian of Myrist
