📋 House Rules

Death's Door

When a player character is reduced to 0 hit points, instead of falling unconscious and making death saving throws as normal, they must immediately choose one of the following three options. The standard death saving throw rules do not apply.

If your character dies, work with the DM before the next session to create a new character at the same level as the rest of the party.

⚔️ Blaze of Glory

Your character embraces death and goes out in a blaze of glory. You take one final action of your choice — an attack, a spell, a shove, anything — and it automatically succeeds as a critical hit or critical success (with DM approval on non-attack actions). After resolving that action, your character dies. No death saving throws, no stabilization. They are gone.

🛡️ Avoid Death

Your character falls unconscious but clings to life. They drop to 0 HP and fall prone, but cannot be targeted by attacks or harmful effects while unconscious this way — enemies will turn their attention elsewhere. Your character regains consciousness and returns to 1 HP when an ally uses a healing spell or ability on them, or when the party completes a long rest.

After falling unconscious, roll a d20:

  • On a 20, your character's will to survive pulls them back — they immediately stand up with 1 HP and do not gain a Scar.
  • On a result less than your character's level, they gain a Scar.
  • On any other result, they simply fall unconscious with no further consequence.

Work with the DM to determine a Scar's lasting narrative effect — a permanent injury, a psychological toll, a lost relationship, or some other consequence. Each Scar is tracked, and some may be healable through specific in-world means determined by the DM.

You can have a number of Scars equal to your Proficiency Bonus. If you are reduced to 0 HP and cannot gain a Scar (because you are already at your maximum), you must choose a different option instead.

🎲 Risk It All

Roll a d20.

  • On a 10 or higher: Your character surges with adrenaline, stays on their feet, and regains a number of HP equal to the result of the roll.
  • On a 9 or lower: Your character crosses the threshold. They die immediately.

Restricted Content

The following content is banned or requires DM approval before use. When in doubt, ask before selecting or preparing anything below.

Banned Spells

These spells are not available in this campaign regardless of class or source.

SpellReason
Silvery BarbsReaction-speed reroll with forced disadvantage — too swingy and removes enemy agency entirely
SimulacrumCreates duplicate casters; breaks action economy at high levels
WishOpen-ended wish magic does not exist in Norrath's cosmology
CloneCircumvents death consequences, undermining core campaign stakes
Heroes' FeastThe immunity to fear and poison and the HP boost make many encounters trivial when reliably prepared
ForcecageNo-save, no-escape imprisonment with no reliable counter at the levels it becomes available

Banned Subclasses

These subclasses are not available for player characters.

SubclassReason
Cleric — Twilight DomainStacks too many immunities and utility features for a single character
Cleric — Peace DomainEmboldening Bond provides a consistent, stackable bonus that warps encounter math

Sourcebook Policy

Core rulebooks (PHB, DMG, MM) and official supplements (Xanathar's Guide, Tasha's Cauldron, Monsters of the Multiverse, etc.) are permitted. Content from any other source — third-party, Unearthed Arcana, Critical Role, or setting-specific books — requires DM approval before character creation or level-up. When in doubt, ask first.
