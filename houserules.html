📋 House Rules

Campaign-specific rules, modifications to the base D&D system, and EverQuest-specific mechanics adapted for tabletop play. These rules are in addition to — or supersede — the standard D&D rules.

Death & Recovery

Experience Debt. When a character dies and is resurrected, they suffer a 5% experience penalty — they begin the session slightly behind where they were when they fell. This is represented mechanically by requiring an additional milestone to be completed before the next level-up trigger, DM's discretion.

Corpse Recovery. When a character dies in a dangerous zone, their body remains where they fell. Resurrection magic brings the character back to life at the nearest safe zone — but their equipment remains on their corpse. The party has until the end of the next in-game day to recover the corpse and reclaim the equipment before it decays and is lost.

Soul Binding. Characters may designate a Soul Bind location — a specific city or safe zone they have previously visited. Upon death without resurrection, the character's soul returns to this location rather than requiring a full resurrection. Characters can change their Soul Bind by meditating at a new location (takes 10 minutes and requires a short rest).

Feign Death (Monk ability): The monk makes a Deception check (DC 12 + enemy's Wisdom modifier). On success, all enemies that can see the monk believe them to be dead and stop targeting them. Enemies that witnessed the monk "die" unrealistically (i.e., at full health) have advantage on Insight to disbelieve.

Travel Rules

Zone Travel. Moving between adjacent zones requires travel time based on terrain. Road travel: 30 minutes per zone. Wilderness travel: 1–2 hours per zone. Dangerous zones (dungeon, high-level outdoor): 1 encounter check per zone entered.

Druid Rings. Druids of level 5+ may use ancient stone circles to teleport themselves and up to 5 willing allies to any other Druid Ring they have previously visited. This requires 1 minute of casting time and a spell slot of appropriate level. Druid Rings exist in: West Karana, East Karana, North Karana, Butcherblock Mountains, Toxxulia Forest, Wakening Land (Velious), and several Kunark locations.

Wizard Translocate. Wizards of level 9+ may translocate a single willing creature to any location the wizard has previously visited and designated as a translocate anchor. This requires a 6th-level spell slot and 1 minute of casting. Mass translocate affects the full party at 7th level slot.

Sea Travel. Passenger ships operate between Freeport–Butcherblock, Freeport–Firiona Vie (Kunark), and Butcherblock–Firiona Vie. Travel time is handled narratively. Ships depart every 2–4 hours in-game. Shipwreck encounters are possible at DM's discretion.

Every major organization in Norrath maintains a Faction Standing toward each character, ranging from Ally (warmly welcomed) to Scowls (attacked on sight). Actions taken by the party affect faction standings.

EverQuest-Specific Rules

Camping a Spawn. When the party waits in a specific location for a named NPC to appear (a "camp"), encounters in that zone occur at accelerated pace. Named NPCs appear on a 1d6 hours schedule; waiting characters may take short rests but not long rests without abandoning the camp.

See Invisible. Many powerful creatures possess magical awareness that defeats mundane invisibility. Divinity-touched entities, powerful undead, and most high-level adversaries can automatically perceive invisible targets within 60 feet. Only creatures with this limitation noted can be fooled by Invisibility spells.

Lull / Mesmerize. Enchant effects that reduce hostility ("lulling" creatures) last only as long as concentration is maintained and break immediately if the affected creature takes damage. Mesmerize (Hold Person/Monster) breaks on damage as normal. Crowd control is valuable but fragile.

Aggro Range. Different creatures have different hostile awareness ranges. Undead and naturally aggressive creatures are hostile at 30 feet. Standard enemies are hostile at 15 feet unless they observe the party. Rare "see-invis" enemies detect invisible creatures within their standard range regardless.

Tradeskills. Characters may specialize in a craft (Smithing, Tailoring, Brewing, Baking, Fletching, Pottery, Jewel Crafting). Crafted goods may exceed the quality available for purchase; legendary crafted items require rare components and high skill. Tradeskill advancement is tracked separately from combat advancement.
